How to Calculate Average Stock Cost

Simulate your final average price and total investment in real-time when buying more shares. This guide explains what the tool calculates or changes, how to use it carefully, and where a quick browser result has limits.

Stock/Crypto Avg Cost Calculator basics

Average cost is a weighted average: add the cost of every purchase, then divide total cost by the total number of shares or units. A simple average of purchase prices is wrong when purchase quantities differ.

For Stock/Crypto Avg Cost Calculator, the useful habit is to connect the displayed answer back to the exact formula, units, and assumptions behind the fields.

How to read the result

  1. Start with the real-world question: cost, rate, time, grade, distance, or another measurable value.
  2. Enter values in the units shown by the fields, then check that percentages are entered as percentages rather than decimals unless the tool says otherwise.
  3. Read both the main answer and any supporting totals. Supporting totals often explain why the final number changed.
  4. Change one input and compare the difference. This is the fastest way to understand which value controls the outcome.
  5. For consequential work, record the inputs along with the answer so the result can be reproduced later.

Practical example

Owning 10 shares bought at $100 and adding 5 shares at $80 produces a total cost of $1,400 across 15 shares. The new average cost is $1,400 / 15, or about $93.33 per share before fees.
